causes of climate change:

GREENHOUSE
GASES

To put it simply, Greenhouse Gases are natural gases that trap heat. They allow sunlight to pass through the atmosphere, but they do not let the heat escape with the light.

In regular amounts, Greenhouse Gases make earth habitable, and provide us with heat. However, human activities have caused a spike in greenhouse gases, leading to global warming.

Why is this happening?

The main gases that contribute to climate change.

Carbon Dioxide

Carbon Dioxide

Carbon Dioxide (CO2) is the greenhouse gas emitted the most through human activities, making up for over 70% of all emissions.

It is most commonly emitted when fossil fuel and natural gases are burned. Therefore, to reduce the amount of carbon dioxide in the air, we need to cut down on amount of fossil fuel used.

Methane

Methane

Methane (CH4) comes from the decomposition of plant matter, especially from forested areas such as rainforests, and rice paddies. It can also come from cattle.

Despite being less commmon than carbon dioxide, it has 25 times the global warming potential as compared to carbon dioxide.

Nitrous Oxide

Nitrous Oxide

Nitrous Oxide (N20) comes from the bacteria in soil, especially during modern agricultural practices such as lifestock management and tilling.

Nitrous oxide has a global warming potential that is 198 times higher than carbon dioxide, which makes it one of the most significant contributors to greenhouse gas emissions.

Clorofluorocarbons
(CFC)

Chloroflourocarbon
(CFC)

Chloroflourocarbons are the most recent of the bunch, coming exclusively from the industrial era.

They are far less common as other greenhouse gases, but are extremely powerful, boasting a global warming potential of up to 10,000. It is also long lasting, able to remain in the atmosphere for 45-100 years. In fact, they are responsible for the destruction of the ozone layer.
